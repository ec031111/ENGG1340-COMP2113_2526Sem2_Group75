## Introduction

This guide assumes you have read the Player Guide and understand the basic game loop. Here we focus on the principles and specific compositions that separate winning play from losing play. Auto-Battler Arena rewards forward planning, economic discipline, and the ability to adapt when your original strategy is not working.

---

## 1. Core Principles

### 1.1 Commit Early, Adapt Late

The single biggest mistake new players make is switching compositions too often. Every time you sell units to switch strategies, you lose gold and lose upgrade progress. In general:

- **Rounds 1–3:** Be flexible. Buy the strongest units available and avoid spending on upgrades for units you are not committed to.  
- **Rounds 4–6:** Commit to a core composition of 4–6 units. Stop buying units outside your primary role distribution.  
- **Rounds 7+:** All purchases should be upgrades or synergy completions. If you are not upgrading, you are falling behind.  

The exception: if you are on a five-round loss streak with no upgrades, switching is often correct even late. A bad composition cannot be saved by more copies of the same units.

### 1.2 Gold Efficiency

Every gold you spend should advance one of three goals:
1. Complete an upgrade (three copies → starred unit)  
2. Reach the next synergy tier (3 Archers → buy a fourth for Pierce)  
3. Reach the next player level (unlock better shop odds)  

Re-rolling for random units in early rounds is almost never worth 2 gold unless you are very close to a specific upgrade.

### 1.3 Reading the Shop

Before spending any gold, scan the entire shop and ask:
- Is there a unit that completes an upgrade for me?  
- Is there a unit that adds a new synergy tier I am currently one unit away from?  
- Is there a unit that significantly improves my weakest position?  

Only if none of these apply should you consider re-rolling.

### 1.4 Loss Management

Losing rounds is not inherently bad. A loss streak of three or more awards bonus gold each round. If you are already losing, lean into it: hold gold, maximise interest, and prepare for a large purchase round when you can afford Tier 2–3 units.

Never panic-upgrade to avoid one loss. The gold spent panic-upgrading is almost always better saved for a larger power spike the next round.

## 2. Composition Archetypes

### 2.1 The Classic Frontline (Beginner-Friendly)

**Core Units:** 2 Tanks + 2 Warriors + 2 Mages or Archers  
**Synergies Active:** Warrior (2-unit), Tank (2-unit)  
**Tier Requirement:** Functional from Tier 1–2  

**How it works:** Tanks hold the front line and absorb damage. Warriors fill the remaining front-line slots and deal moderate damage. Mages or Archers sit safely in Row 2 and deal ranged damage to the enemy backline.

**Strengths:**
- Very stable and forgiving. Hard to lose catastrophically.  
- Easy to upgrade any individual unit without disrupting the composition.  
- Consistent win rate against random or unoptimised opponents.  

**Weaknesses:**
- Maximum potential is below that of specialized compositions at Tier 3.  
- Vulnerable to high-burst Assassin compositions that bypass the Tank frontline.  
- The composition does not improve dramatically with player level — late game it falls behind compositions that activate 4-unit synergy tiers.  

**Upgrade Path:** Prioritise upgrading Tanks first (they absorb the most damage), then Mages (highest damage output growth from upgrade). Upgrade Warriors last — they gain the least proportional benefit.

---

### 2.2 Full Mage (Glass Cannon)

**Core Units:** 4 Mages + 2 Tanks (as protective frontline)  
**Synergies Active:** Mage (4-unit), Tank (2-unit)  
**Tier Requirement:** Requires Tier 2 Mages to function; peaks at Tier 3  

**How it works:** Four Mages in the backline with splash damage decimates enemy frontlines simultaneously. Two Tanks in Row 1 exist solely to absorb enough damage for the Mages to complete their work.

**Round-by-Round Plan:**
- Round 1–4: Field whatever frontline units you can afford. Buy every Mage that appears in the shop.  
- Round 5–6: Aim for 3 Mages on the board. The 2-unit bonus alone is a significant upgrade.  
- Round 7+: Achieve 4 Mages + 4-unit synergy. Each attack now splashes two units on either side.  

**Strengths:**
- Best total damage output of any composition at 4-unit activation with Tier 2+ Mages.  
- Splash damage hits multiple targets simultaneously, making it devastating against bunched Warriors or Tanks.  
- Relatively straightforward to execute — no complex positioning decisions.  

**Weaknesses:**
- Extremely vulnerable to Assassin compositions. A starred Nightblade or Phantom Sovereign one-shots every Mage in the lineup.  
- Requires significant gold investment (Mages cost 1–3 gold each, multiple copies needed).  
- The 2-Tank frontline provides inadequate defense against Warriors; supplementing with a third Tank dilutes the Mage synergy.  

**Counter-play:** If you suspect your opponent is running 4 Mages, field a fast Assassin in your backline. When the Assassin jumps to the enemy backline at combat start, they target the highest-damage enemy — typically a Tier 3 Mage — and have a chance to eliminate it before splash damage becomes overwhelming.

---

### 2.3 Assassin Strike Force (High Skill Ceiling)

**Core Units:** 4 Assassins + 2 flexible frontline units  
**Synergies Active:** Assassin (4-unit)  
**Tier Requirement:** Requires Tier 2 Assassins; Tier 3 is the win condition  

**How it works:** All four Assassins jump to the enemy backline at combat start. With bleed active (4-unit Assassin synergy), every crit leaves the target bleeding for 3 turns. In an optimal scenario, four Assassins simultaneously eliminating four enemy backline units ends the combat instantly.

**Strengths:**
- Highest burst damage ceiling in the game with Phantom Sovereign.  
- Ignores Tank positioning — Assassins jump over the frontline entirely.  
- Win-condition is extremely fast; combats often end in 2–3 turns.  

**Weaknesses:**
- High-HP targets (Fortress Titan) survive the initial burst and can fight back.  
- Relies on critical hit RNG; without crits, Assassin damage is merely above average, not game-winning.  
- If the Assassins are eliminated in the enemy backline before the rest of the enemy team is killed, the player is left with only the 2 frontline units.  

**Positioning Note:** Place your 2 frontline units in Rows 1 columns 2 and 3 (centre). Leave the edge columns empty. This ensures that enemy frontline units spend one extra turn navigating to your backline, buying the Assassins time to do their work.

---

### 2.4 Archer Sniper Line (Sustained DPS)

**Core Units:** 4 Archers + 2 Tanks  
**Synergies Active:** Archer (4-unit), Tank (2-unit)  
**Tier Requirement:** Works from Tier 1 but peaks at Tier 2 with Hawk Rangers  

**How it works:** Four Archers in Row 2 with Pierce attack hit both the frontline target and the unit directly behind it. Two Tanks in Row 1 absorb melee damage and taunt enemies. The Archers deal steady damage to both rows of the enemy team simultaneously.

**Round-by-Round Plan:**
- Round 1–3: Buy every Archer available. Field 2 Archers + 1 Tank minimum.  
- Round 4–5: Achieve 4-unit Archer synergy. Pierce activates.  
- Round 6+: Focus on upgrading Archers to Tier 2 (Hawk Ranger). Each upgrade dramatically increases sustained damage.  

**Strengths:**
- Extremely consistent damage output. No RNG dependency (no crit reliance).  
- Pierce means the Tanks cannot fully protect the enemy backline.  
- Strong against Warrior and Tank compositions that stack units in Row 1 (Pierce chains hit multiple front-row units).  

**Weaknesses:**
- Takes many turns to kill high-HP opponents; vulnerable to quick burst compositions.  
- Enemy Assassins bypass the Tanks entirely and target the Archers, who have moderate HP.  
- Re-rolling for 4 Archers in the shop is expensive. The pool has fewer copies of each unit than Tier 1 units.  

---

### 2.5 Mixed Frontline Counter-Build (Adaptive Strategy)

This is not a fixed composition but a mindset: choose your units based on what you can observe about the current meta or opponent patterns.

If you consistently face Mage-heavy opponents: increase Tank count (shield absorbs splash) + add Assassins to jump their backline.  
If you consistently face Assassin-heavy opponents: increase your back-row HP (field one Warrior in Row 2 as an Assassin sponge) + add Warriors in Row 1 to intercept any Assassins that don't jump cleanly.  
If you consistently face Tank-heavy opponents: switch to Mage splash or Archer Pierce, both of which bypass single-target damage limitations.

## 3. Economic Strategies

### 3.1 Level 1–3: Economy Phase

**Goal:** End Round 3 with 40+ gold.  

- Buy only units that directly contribute to your planned composition.  
- Do not re-roll at all during Rounds 1–2.  
- Accept losses if the shop does not offer useful units; do not buy filler units just to have more units on the board.  
- Sell any units that are clearly off-composition.  

### 3.2 Level 4–5: Transition Phase

**Goal:** Begin completing upgrades; achieve first 2-unit synergy bonus.  

- Start spending some gold on re-rolls if you are one unit away from an upgrade.  
- Buy XP to reach Level 5, unlocking improved shop odds.  
- Hold a minimum of 30 gold during this phase for interest income.  

### 3.3 Level 6+: Power Phase

**Goal:** Achieve 4-unit synergy bonus; upgrade key units to starred tier.  

- This is the phase where your economic investment pays off. Spend gold freely on high-impact purchases.  
- Prioritise starred upgrades of your highest-attack or highest-defense unit.  
- If you reach Level 7 or 8, Tier 3 units appear frequently in the shop. Save for Phantom Sovereign or Void Sorcerer if your composition supports them.

## 4. Reading Combat to Improve

After every loss, spend 10 seconds reviewing the combat log. Ask:
1. Which unit died first on my side?  
2. What killed it?  
3. What does that tell me about my composition's weakness?  

**Common patterns and fixes:**

| Observation | Likely Problem | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Mages die on Turn 1 | Enemy Assassins jumping backline | Add a frontline unit in Row 2, or field a Tank with taunt closer to your Mages |
| Warriors die before reaching enemies | Enemy Archers outrange them | Add more Warriors (Warrior synergy HP regen) or a Tank (higher defense) |
| All units die quickly | Enemy composition is 2 tiers ahead | Prioritise upgrades; re-roll aggressively next round |
| Tank survives but everyone else dies | Frontline is fine, backline is underperforming | Upgrade a Mage or Archer; check synergy counts |
| Combat goes to time limit, you lose | Neither side deals enough damage | Switch to higher-attack role; Mage splash helps in this scenario |

## 5. Frequently Overlooked Mechanics

### 5.1 The Minimum Damage Rule

Every attack deals at least **1 damage**, even if the attacker's attack stat is lower than the target's defense. This means even a Tier 1 unit with low attack will eventually kill a Fortress Titan — it just takes a very long time. Do not leave your frontline empty just because your attackers seem weak against a heavily armored opponent.

### 5.2 Synergy Counts Include Only Board Units

A unit on the **bench** does not count toward synergy activation. If you have 3 Archers total but only 2 are deployed on the board, only the 2-unit Archer synergy is active — not the 4-unit tier. Always check synergy counts relative to your deployed board, not your total roster.

### 5.3 The Re-Roll Odds Shift With Player Level

Re-rolling at Level 3 heavily favors Tier 1 units. Re-rolling at Level 6 gives a 40% chance of Tier 2 and a 15% chance of Tier 3. If you are hunting for a Tier 3 unit, do not re-roll until you have reached at least Level 6 — you are unlikely to see it earlier.

### 5.4 Event Round Preparation

Events are announced at the end of rounds 3, 6, and 9. Before you finish the planning phase of those rounds, think about whether the upcoming event might favour or disfavour your current plan. If a Mage debuff event is announced and you run 4 Mages, consider temporarily benching one Mage and adding a Warrior to your board — losing the 4-unit Mage synergy for one round is better than playing with a severely weakened composition.

### 5.5 Sell Timing

If you are going to sell a unit, sell it **before** buying the unit you actually want. This frees up bench space and gives you the gold immediately, potentially letting you afford the replacement unit in the same round.

## 6. Win Conditions and End-Game Checklist

By Round 8, a winning composition typically looks like:

- ✅ At least one 4-unit synergy active  
- ✅ At least two starred units on the board  
- ✅ No empty front-line positions (Row 1 fully covered)  
- ✅ The highest-damage unit (Mage or Archer) is in Row 2  
- ✅ At least 5 gold held for base interest  

If any of these are not true by Round 8, assess which is most achievable in the planning phase and prioritise it.
